Acquisition

  • Starting armament of the Vagabond origin.
  • Purchased from Twin Maiden Husks at the Roundtable Hold for 1,000 Runes.

Notes

  • The default skill is useful for breaking the guard of blocking enemies with the light attack version or for breaking their Stance with the heavy attack version.
  • Consistently the second strongest of all regular Straight Swords, outmatched only by the Broadsword, almost matching it on a Keen affinity and outclassing it on a Quality affinity.
    • This may be somewhat surprising since the Longsword's scaling values leans towards Strength more so than Dexterity, but it's primarily because the Broadsword scales rather less well on Keen.
    • Quality affinity requires high stats to use properly, making this somewhat difficult to exploit before New Game Plus/Journey 2 and later.
  • While not quite to the level of the Short Sword's unique heavy attack chain, since the first heavy attack in a chain is a Pierce damage attack as well as the heavy variant of its default skill and as such can hit for heavy counter damage with the Spear Talisman.


Upgrade Information

  • Upgraded with regular Smithing Stones.
  • Can have its Affinity and Skill changed.
  • All non-physical Affinities lower all types of guarded damage negation by 5%, except for the associated element where the damage negation increases by 25% and another element also increases by 15%.
    • Magic negation receives the secondary 15% negation bonus on Sacred and Cold affinities, Fire negation gets the increase on Lightning affinity, Lightning negation gets the increase on Fire and Flame Art affinities, and Holy negation gets the increase on Magic affinity.
  • The upper number in the Atk column denotes Physical base damage while the lower, colored number in the Atk column denotes elemental base damage: red for Fire damage, yellow for Lightning damage, orange for Holy damage, and cyan for Magic damage.
  • See the Weapon Scaling page for what the scaling values mean.
